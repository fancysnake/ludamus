Based on the code and visual design tokens, I would describe this style as "Grimdark Luxury" or "Cinematic Tabletop."

It combines the grit of underground roleplaying games with the elegance of high-end editorial design. Here is a breakdown of the specific elements that create this look:

1. The "Gilded Void" Color Palette

Deepest Darks: Instead of pure black, it uses rich, warm charcoals (#0c0a09, #1a1614) to create a soft, immersive background that feels like a dimly lit room.

Antique Gold: The primary accent is a muted, metallic gold (#c5a059), used sparingly for borders and buttons, evoking brass fittings or gold-leaf lettering on a book cover.

Parchment Text: Text isn't stark white; it is off-white/bone (#f0e6d2), mimicking the page of an old novel or rulebook to reduce eye strain.

2. Editorial Serif Typography The choice of Marauder is the defining feature.

It moves away from the standard "tech" look (San Francisco/Inter) and embraces a literary aesthetic.

The heavy use of uppercase with wide letter spacing (tracking-widest) gives headers a cinematic, movie-poster quality.

3. Atmospheric Texture

Digital Noise: The NoiseOverlay component adds a layer of film grain/static. This prevents the flat colors from looking too "digital" or sterile, adding a tactile, gritty feel.

Glassmorphism: The use of backdrop-blur on the navigation and cards adds a modern layer of depth, making the UI feel like glass floating over a dark abyss.

4. Immersive Layouts

Cinematic Cards: The UI relies on large, full-width imagery with aggressive gradients that fade into the background color. This minimizes the "boxiness" of the web page, making images feel like they are emerging from the darkness.
